Rua Hamid

Rua Hamid comes from Brooklyn, NY and graduated from Brooklyn Academy of Science and the Environment (B.A.S.E) High school in 2015. Now as an undergraduate student at Queen College (QC), Rua is a geology B.A major with a minor in studio arts. She currently works with professor Dax Soule in his research lab developing and utilizing hard skills and learning geophysics. Rua has also done mineralogical research with Jacquelyn Bracco in QC as well as, research at several other institutions such as, NYC’s College of Technology’s and Texas A&M in Texas and Costa Rica. From 2015-2019 she was the president of Gen ’19 of the QC The Percy Ellis Sutton Search for Education, Elevation, and Knowledge (SEEK)Program. She is now the founder of We are STEM (WSTEM), a nonprofit that aims to inspire, network and enhance NYC’s minorities in STEM. After graduation Rua plans to continue her nonprofit and eventually start other businesses involving STEM.

Jazmyn Fuller

Jazmyn Fuller is a first-generation college student from Queens, New York. She recently graduated from Queens College in Fall 2019 with her BS in Computer Science, and minors in Math, Music, and Interaction Design. Jazmyn's focuses include Web Development, and Data Engineering/Visualization. During her final year as an undergrad, she completed CUNY Tech Prep's competitive Fullstack Web development program, became a finalist for NCWIT's Collegiate Award, received full scholarship for the largest computing event for women in the world, the Grace Hopper Celebration, and worked under PhD. Dax Soule in his research lab. As an alumni (Summer '11) of the GOALS Summer Intensive Program, Jazmyn goes back to Intrepid to encourage young women to explore STEM, and apply for the 6-week summer program that helped her get to where she is today. On the side, Jazmyn teaches piano at her childhood music school, volunteers as a hospitality member in her church, and enjoys traveling. Jazmyn recently received an offer for a government job in Automation that she will start after returning from her expedition with the Neanderthal Lab.

Jacqueline Sarimento

Jacqueline Sarmiento is a first-generation college student who currently attends CUNY Queens College. She is a senior working towards her B.A. in biology and minor(s) in chemistry and psychology. Before attending CUNY Queens College, she graduated from CUNY Queensborough Community College with an Associates in Liberal Arts/ Science and Mathematics. She is currently a general biology I peer mentor at CUNY Queensborough Community College. As a peer mentor, she helps out in the lab section of the course and holds her own office hours for students who need help. Outside of school, she does Congestive Heart Failure clinical research, as well as patient care volunteering at New York Presbyterian Brooklyn Methodist Hospital. Lastly, she does community service at an organization called ‘Spoons Across America’ where she encourages and educates kids on better food choices. On her free time, she likes to powerlift at the gym. After graduation, she plans to take a gap year and then hopes to apply to graduate/medical school.
