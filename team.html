Meethila Rahman

B.S. in Environmental Science

Meethila Rahman is a graduate from Queens College having pursued a B.S. in Environmental Science. Her interests lie in science communication and analyzing/interpreting data for stories to make positive change. Her undergraduate project studied the origination of floc events (loose fine bacterial particles erupting like "snow") in harsh deep-sea volcanic environments that she had the wonderful blessing to witness on the Visions '18 expedition to Axial Seamount, Pacific Ocean. She also enjoyed being a peer mentor and lab TA for students in introduction Geology/Env Sci classes to spark interest in students to learn about our planet in order to love and preserve it. After graduation, she works as a digital marketing specialist with hopes to use her skills to continue her mission to spark knowledge about the Earth for its preservation on a larger public scale. She also enjoys spending quality time with her family, swimming, hiking and exploring new places.

Alumni:

Elizabeth Pesar

BSc. in Geology
Minor: Math

Elizabeth graduated Summa Cum Laude from Queens College in 2019 earning a BSc. in Geology and Math minor and won the Lt. George C. Gierak Memorial Award from the SEES department. At Queens College, she worked as a Geology Peer Mentor, and as a Geology and Environmental Science tutor. She has worked on visualizing data trends at Axial Seamount volcano, studied the geochemical evolution of Arabia Terra, Mars as a NASA intern, and worked on chondrite meteorite projects at both Queens College and Tokyo Institute of Technology, Japan. She has presented her work at the AGU and LPSC conferences. She will begin her PhD at the University of Florida in Fall 2020 studying the isotope geochemistry of moon rocks, peridotites, and planetary accretion and formation processes. Following her doctorate, she hopes to complete a post doc, or begin a job focused on research or industry. In her free time, she enjoys crochet, hiking, and video games.

Aida Gonzalez

B.S Environmental Science


Aida Gonzalez is a first-generation student who transferred to CUNY Queens College from LaGuardia Community College in 2018. While at Queens College, Aida did research with professor Dax Soule in the Neanderthal Lab in conjunction with professor Karen Bemis at Rutgers University visualizing data trends from hydrothermal vents in the Axial Seamount volcano at the Juan de Fuca ridge collected by the Cabled Observatory Vent Imaging Sonar System (COVIS). She created 3D images utilizing her newly acquired programming skills in MatLab and Python. Her role in the research was to produce 3D images that will allow further interpretation of the different dynamics around the hydrothermal vents. She graduated from CUNY Queens College in 2020 with a B.S in Environmental Science. After graduation she hopes to work in the coastal and marine science sector in Europe.
